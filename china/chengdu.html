China: Chengdu

Landscapes

The Dujiangyan Scenic Area is located along the main branch of the Lancang River, west of Dujiangyan City. It was constructed in the Qin dynasty around 256 BC by Li Bing, Prefecture Chief of Shu County, and his son. It is one of the few ancient Chinese water conservancy projects that has remained preserved and intact.

The Chengdu Research Base of Giant Panda Breeding is located in the northern suburbs of Chengdu City, about 10 kilometers away. The facility simulates the natural habitat of the giant pandas, and has lakes, streams, bamboo forests, and lawns. The ability to come into close contact with the giant pandas is a feature here, visitors can have a close-up observation of the pandas' living conditions. There is also a "Panda Villa", which is a restricted area for the pandas to rest.

Kuanzhai Alley is an ancient Qing Dynasty street left over from Chengdu. It is composed of three lanes of city-style streets arranged in parallel, a wide alleyway, a narrow alleyway, a well alleyway, with a courtyard community between them. Here, you may drink tea, eat hotpot, and feel the leisurely slow new lifestyle in Chengdu. You can have a cup of tea here and make small talk with the old men next to you; you can also comfortably soak in the atmosphere. Kuanzhai Alley at night is also full of charm, Dianzui, Baiye, Huli, and other bars are available, providing a feel of modernity, full of fashion sense.
