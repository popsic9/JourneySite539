Japan: Tokyo

Landscapes

The Imperial Palace is the primary residence of the Emperor of Japan. Located in the Chiyoda district of central Tokyo, it was built in 1590 by the order of shogun Tokugawa Ieyasu. The inner grounds of the palace are not generally open to the public, except for certain days of the year. However, visitors are free to browse the Imperial Palace Outer Gardens and the East Gardens, or even take photos of Nijubashi Bridge.

Tokyo Tower Built in 1958, the Tokyo Tower was modeled after the Eiffel Tower in Paris, and is a must-see in Tokyo. There is an observatory at different levels that provide a 360-degree view of the city. The Tokyo Tower frequently appears in film and television dramas, novels, music, etc., especially the red and white iron towers illuminated by the evening light, which is a scene often seen in Japanese TV dramas

Ueno Park An extremely popular park in Tokyo, Ueno Park boasts a wide range of attractions such as art galleries, museums, and a zoo. Visitors are free to wonder around and experience the natural beauty and culture of this place. Every spring, visitors pour in to this famous hanami spot to view the sea of cherry blossoms within. Indeed, the falling cherry blossoms in the breeze is a truly spectacular sight.
